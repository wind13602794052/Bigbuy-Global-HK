設計與優化案例

Design & Optimization — connecting rod motion

Product: 連桿運動結構 · Connecting rod movement structure

優化前 · Before optimization

優化後 · After optimization

Position 1

  • Ribs and end-face strength were insufficient — risk of cracking and bending. Improvement: add ribs, increase wall thickness, enlarge connections.
  • Screw installation space was insufficient; screws had to tilt. Improvement: add clearance and adjustment features for reliable fastening.

Position 2

  • No mechanical positioning; all screw-fixed — bearing block needed high strength but screw fixation was weak. Improvement: add positioning stops on the first accessory; ensure accuracy.
  • Accessory 2 screw direction opposed the main body. Improvement: align assembly direction after customer alignment; unify screw sizes where possible to save time and fastener cost.

Position 3

  • Similar positioning issue on another component — add stop features and guarantee accuracy.
  • Two screws insufficient — increase to four for fixation strength.

改善方案與效果 · Improvement plan & effect

A. Strengthen thin areas of accessory 1 → better quality; fewer design iterations (−1 round).

B. Two screw fixing blocks stabilised → transmission quality and reliability up; development iterations reduced by 1–3 rounds.

C. Consistent screw assembly direction and verified gaps → higher assembly efficiency; fewer iterations (−1).

Client satisfaction: project development cycle expected to shorten by >3 weeks; development cost ~−20%; production & assembly cost ~−15%.

成本優勢 · Cost Advantages

Cycle · Tooling · Supply chain · Factory direct

Development cycle & iteration

Strong structural development capability typically closes appearance and assembly issues within about two major revision cycles — saving time and engineering cost versus open-loop trial production.

Reference case (connecting rod structure): measurable cycle and cost improvements as quoted in the optimisation section above.

Mold & tooling economics

Examples from our practice: 1600T class die-casting mold quoted around €35,000 at factory pricing level; multi-cavity / multi-product on one mold where feasible; optional shared mold frame strategies for lower capital outlay (with agreed precision trade-offs).

Supply chain

Mature, responsive supplier network in the Pearl River Delta for materials, secondary processes (coating, anodising, etc.) and quick-turn components.

Factory direct supply

Injection moulding, die casting, stamping and related processes sourced at direct factory pricing, reducing layers of margin and communication overhead.
